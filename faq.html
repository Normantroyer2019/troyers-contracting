A properly installed metal roof can last 40 to 70 years or more, depending on the material and environmental conditions. Standing seam roofs tend to last the longest due to their concealed fastener system. Compare this to traditional asphalt shingles, which typically last only 15 to 25 years. With minimal maintenance, a metal roof is truly a lifetime investment for your property.

Metal roofing offers numerous advantages over traditional roofing materials:

  • Longevity: 40–70+ year lifespan
  • Durability: Resists wind, hail, fire, and rot
  • Energy efficiency: Reflects solar heat, reducing cooling costs by up to 25%
  • Low maintenance: No shingles to replace or patch
  • Eco-friendly: 100% recyclable at end of life
  • Curb appeal: Wide variety of styles and colors
  • Increased home value: Metal roofs can increase resale value

The cost of a metal roof varies depending on several factors: the type of metal (standing seam vs. corrugated), the size and complexity of your roof, your location, and the specific materials chosen. Generally, metal roofing costs more upfront than asphalt shingles, but the long-term savings in maintenance, energy costs, and replacement make it a more cost-effective choice over time.

This is one of the most common myths about metal roofing. When properly installed with solid sheathing (plywood or OSB decking) and underlayment — which is standard practice — a metal roof is no louder than any other roofing material during rain. Many homeowners are surprised at how quiet their metal roof actually is. The layers of decking and insulation beneath the panels effectively dampen any sound.
